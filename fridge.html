For my final project in my Intro to HCI class, my team developed a web application prototype that enables users to organize their fridge by prioritizing foods by expiration dates. Each user has a fridge list that contains all the items in their fridge organized by expiration date; users have the option of scanning a grocery receipt and letting our app populate the food and respective expiration date, or they can manually add items and expiration dates for meals that may not have concrete data (ex: takeout). The app additionally allows users to meal-prep by providing relevant recipes per their dietary restrictions. The goal of the app is to minimize food waste and save money, as well as simplify the process of meal prep.


Our target audience is primarily busy parents and students living off campus, but the app can aid anyone who prepares food. We conducted user research by interviewing local parents and students living off campus, and the feedback we received from our target audience drove every iteration cycle. The high-fidelity prototype is designed using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I was involved in every step of the process, from interviews, building the low fidelity prototype, and coding portions of the high fidelity prototype.
