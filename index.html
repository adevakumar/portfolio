Welcome to my website!

I'm a hardworking student passionate about the intersections of human-computer interaction, visual psychology, and computing for good to create thoughtful user experiences.

I recently graduated from the University of Massachusetts, Amherst with a B.S in Computer Science and a Psychology minor. I will be attending Georgia Tech for Masters in Human-Computer Interaction in Fall 2019.

Background

In college, I have taken Psychology, Communication Disorders, and Linguistics classes in addition to Computer Science; this broad exposure has led to an interest in Human-Computer Interaction. My primary research goals include understanding how people process information, form decisions and engage with technology to build impactful tools. Understanding how people form judgments and perceive the world can provide insight into how people will interact with interfaces; overall, psychology lends a deeper understanding of human cognition and design. Communication Disorders is an area of interest as well; from personal experiences of living with neurologically impaired family members, another goal is to design accessible educational technology and ensure a helpful, stress-free experience for them.


I plan on attending graduate school in Human-Computer Interaction with the eventual goal of transitioning to industry, and I aim to enrich my knowledge of human behavior, cognitive processes, and technology. Currently, I am working in the Visual Cognition and Attention lab under Professor Kyle Cave to gain research experience, and I am in the process of writing my honors thesis in the areas of HCI, Security and Visual Psychology with cognitive psychology Professor Cave, HCI professor Narges Mahyar, and computer science professor Tim Richards.


My non-academic hobbies include music (both attending concerts & playing the piano and the ukulele), baking healthy desserts, working out, or searching for the perfect cup of coffee!
