About

In college, I have taken Psychology, Communication Disorders, and Linguistics classes in addition to Computer Science; this diverse exposure has led to an interest in Human-Computer Interaction. My primary research goals include understanding how people process information, form decisions and engage with technology in order to build effective tools. Understanding how people form judgments and perceive the world can provide insight into how people will interact with interfaces; overall, psychology lends a deeper understanding of human cognition and design. Communication Disorders is an area of interest as well; from personal experiences of living with autistic and neurologically impaired family members, another goal is to design accessible educational technology and ensure an accommodating, stress-free experience for them.

I plan on attending graduate school Human-Computer Interaction, hoping to enrich my knowledge of human behavior, cognitive processes, and technology. Currently, I am working in the VCA lab under Professor Kyle Cave to gain research experience and I am in the process of writing my honors thesis.

My non-academic hobbies include music (attending concerts & playing the piano and the ukulele), baking healthy desserts, working out, or searching for the perfect cup of coffee!
