For our final project, my Digital Humanities Honors class was challenged to pick a personal problem that would benefit from using smart, immersive technology such as Google Glasses and the Structure Sensor. Inspired by my background in classical piano and my frustration with the current state of online music lessons when I moved away for college, I chose to explore how to simulate a traditional piano lesson in an online environment. A major drawback of online forms of teaching is that they do not offer both an aerial perspective of the teacher playing the keys and a full body view of the teacher. I used a Google Glass to recreate a real-time lesson featuring an aerial playing perspective, and I used a Structure Sensor to model the piano keyboard and my body posture to provide examples of a proper playing posture. With these tools, I aimed to create an experience that would mimic the interactivity of a face-to-face lesson, yet still hold the flexibility of an online setting.
